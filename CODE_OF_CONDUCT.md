# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Using welcoming and inclusive language**
- **Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences**
- **Gracefully accepting constructive criticism**
- **Focusing on what is best for the community**
- **Showing empathy towards other community members**
- **Helping others learn and grow**
- **Providing thoughtful and constructive feedback**
- **Acknowledging and celebrating contributions**

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior by participants include:

- **Harassment** or discriminatory language or actions
- **Personal attacks** or insulting comments
- **Trolling** or deliberately provoking negative reactions
- **Public or private harassment**
- **Publishing others' private information** without explicit permission
- **Spam** or irrelevant content
- **Threats** or intimidation
- **Unwelcome sexual attention** or advances
- **Disruptive behavior** that interferes with productive discussion

## Our Responsibilities

### Project Maintainers

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

Project maintainainers have the right and responsibility to:

- **Remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions** that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct
- **Temporarily or permanently ban** any contributor for behaviors that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful
- **Communicate clearly** with contributors about any moderation actions taken

### Community Members

All community members are expected to:

- **Read and follow this Code of Conduct**
- **Report any violations** to the project maintainers
- **Help maintain a positive and inclusive environment**
- **Assume good faith** in interactions with others

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies both within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project or its community. Examples of representing a project or community include using an official project e-mail address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event. Representation of a project may be further defined and clarified by project maintainers.

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to the project maintainers. You can reach us through:

- **GitHub Issues**: Create an issue with the "Code of Conduct" label
- **Email**: [Add project email here]
- **Direct Message**: Contact any maintainer directly

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and confidentially.

### Enforcement Process

1. **Initial Response**: Project maintainers will acknowledge receipt of the report within 48 hours
2. **Investigation**: We will investigate the reported behavior thoroughly
3. **Action**: We will determine appropriate action based on the severity and context
4. **Follow-up**: We will communicate the outcome to the reporter (confidentially)

### Possible Actions

Depending on the severity and nature of the violation, actions may include:

- **Private warning** about the behavior
- **Public apology** request
- **Temporary suspension** from project activities
- **Permanent ban** from the project
- **Removal of content** that violates the Code of Conduct

## Additional Guidelines for FlowFi/LiquidFlow

### Technical Discussions

- **Be patient** with developers at all skill levels
- **Provide constructive feedback** on code and ideas
- **Explain technical concepts** clearly when helping others
- **Respect different approaches** to solving problems

### DeFi and Financial Context

- **Maintain professional discourse** when discussing financial topics
- **Avoid making financial advice** or guarantees
- **Be transparent about conflicts of interest**
- **Respect user privacy** and security considerations

### Open Source Collaboration

- **Welcome first-time contributors** and help them navigate the project
- **Recognize and appreciate** all forms of contribution (code, docs, feedback, etc.)
- **Follow established processes** for contributions and reviews
- **Celebrate community achievements** and milestones
